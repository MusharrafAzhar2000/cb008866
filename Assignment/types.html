The Main Categories in Tea

Tea is divided into different categories by the way they are plucked and brewed. Even though there are different types of tea and all of them taste different they come from the same plant, Camellia Sinensis. there are four main categories in Tea

  • White Tea
  • Black Tea
  • Green Tea
  • Oolong Tea

White Tea

In Sri Lanka White tea is commonly known Silver Tips,and it's not a very common tea type among Sri Lankans. Silver Tips is not a middle class beverage in Sri Lanka. White tea is made from handpicked buds of the Camellia Sinensis Plant. What makes this Tea rare is even though it is plucked from the Same tea plant. The plucking of these tea buds requires lot of experience, because the person who plucks the tea has to be really gentle so that person doesn't damage the bud and cause fermentation inside the bud, because fermentation causes bitterness.these undamaged buds are lightly baked in filtered sunlight before packing them and transporting them.

Black Tea

Black Tea is the most common tea type in Sri Lanka. Most people in Sri Lanka Drink this Tea Atleast three times a day. Black tea is one of the popular beverages that doesn't have any class difference. pretty much everyone in Sri Lanka drinks Black Tea.Black Tea is picked from the leaves of the shrub of Camellia sinensis and withered,rolled ,fermented and dried before packing. Black Tea is Stronger in flavour than other teas.

Green Tea

Green tea is made from the leaves and buds of Camellia sinensis plant that haven't withered and oxidized as much as black and oolong teas. there are different types of green teas. Tea is quite popular in young adults in Sri Lanka Because it helps with weight loss and it has some good effects on skin after drinking. Green Tea is picked,withered,heated,rolled and then then dried before packing

Oolong tea

Oolong tea is made using semi oxidized leaves from Camellia sinensis plant. Even though this tea is not popular among Sri Lankans, it's quite popular among Western countries and East asian countries for it's refreshning flavour and Aroma. oolong tea originated from China. But other countries also now make Oolong tea. and Sri lanka is one of them.
